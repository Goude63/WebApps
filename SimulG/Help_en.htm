Suggested activities
Retrograde motion example: Select System: "Terrestrial planets", then in the menu "Center" Choose "Earth". Make sure orbits are displayed with the '~' checkbox checked. More info on retrograde motion.
Gravity assist examples: Select System: "Apollo" or "Binary star system". For the binary star system, a strong gravity assist occurs at simulation time=10 months, and another near 2 years. To reduce the waiting time, you can accelerate the simulation speed ([Ctrl] wheel up) when the planet is not to close to a star. Slow down when the planet comes near a star again, otherwise major computational errors will lead to unpredictable results.
For the "Apollo" system, after simulation time reaches a month, the command/service module (csm) comes back near the moon. Here again, you can accelerate simulation speed when the csm is not too close to the Moon or the Earth. The csm will exit earth orbit. You may then select Center: "Sun", zoom out and accelerate simulation (a lot). More on gravity assist. And, more on Apollo free return trajectory.
